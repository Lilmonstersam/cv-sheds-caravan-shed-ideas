Before You Choose a Caravan Shed Design

Most people looking for caravan storage sheds are trying to solve a mix of protection, access and space issues. The common mistake is designing around the caravan body length only, then forgetting the drawbar, spare wheel, roof-mounted air conditioning, solar panels, awning, mirrors and jockey wheel clearance.

HeightMeasure to the highest roof accessory, then allow extra clearance for the door opening, beams and future upgrades.
LengthInclude the drawbar, rear bar, spare wheel and any rear-mounted accessories.
WidthAllow room for doors, mirrors, awning access and safe reversing.
AccessCheck turning space, driveway slope, gate width and approach angle before finalising shed position.
ProtectionDecide whether you need open shelter, partial walling or a fully enclosed caravan garage.
ApprovalsLarger caravan sheds often need site-specific engineering and council approval.
Test the proportions before you commit.

FAQ: Caravan Shed Ideas and Planning

What size shed do I need for a caravan?

It depends on the full travel size of the caravan, not just the body length. Measure from the tip of the drawbar to the rear-most accessory, then add room to walk around the van and close the door.

Is a caravan carport enough protection?

For many owners, yes. A high-clearance caravan carport can protect against harsh sun, rain, hail and falling debris while keeping access simple.

Should I choose a gable, skillion or flat roof?

Gable roofs suit classic shed designs and acreage properties. Skillion roofs can look more modern and help manage water direction on narrow blocks. Flat roof carports can work well where a clean, low-profile appearance is important.

Do caravan sheds need council approval?

Many caravan sheds and larger carports will need approval, particularly when they are tall, close to boundaries or located in areas with specific wind, fire or planning requirements.
